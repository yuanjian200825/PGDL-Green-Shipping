Physics-Guided Deep Learning Framework
for Green Shipping Digital Twins

Background & Challenges

Dilemma of Data & Mechanism Integration

Data Heterogeneity

Addressing the spatiotemporal misalignment between AIS trajectory data (Lagrangian perspective) and meteorological reanalysis data (Eulerian perspective) by establishing precise assimilation mapping to eliminate interpolation errors.

Physical Consistency

Purely data-driven models lack hydrodynamic constraints. This study constructs a Surrogate Ground Truth incorporating environmental added resistance effects to provide physics-compliant labels for model training.

Non-Stationary Adaptation

Actual sea conditions are highly dynamic. By introducing physics-guided mechanisms, the model improves prediction accuracy and generalization under complex wind and wave conditions, overcoming the limitations of black-box models.

Methodology

Dual-Layer Coupling Architecture

The framework comprises a bottom-layer physics-guided data generation engine and a top-layer physics-aware sequence model.

Step 1: Spatiotemporal Assimilation

Applying the k-d tree algorithm to achieve spatiotemporal alignment of multi-source data, reconstructing the instantaneous environmental field during navigation to provide high-precision input features for dynamic analysis.

Step 2: Surrogate Ground Truth

Based on classical ship resistance formulas and stochastic environmental perturbation models, generating high-fidelity energy consumption data containing physical mechanisms to solve the scarcity of high-frequency energy labels.

Step 3: Transformer Modeling

Designing a Transformer network with explicitly embedded physical priors, utilizing self-attention mechanisms to capture long-range temporal dependencies and effectively model the nonlinear hysteresis effects caused by wind and waves.

Robustness Analysis

Stability in Non-Stationary Environments

Addressing sensor noise and sudden sea conditions in actual shipping, ablation experiments and comparative analyses verify the strong robustness of the model in dynamic nonlinear environments.

Noise Interference Resistance

Experiments show that even with high-frequency random fluctuations in input data, the Transformer model effectively filters noise interference, accurately capturing the main trend of energy consumption changes, demonstrating signal extraction capabilities superior to LSTM and GRU.

Extreme Sea Condition Generalization

In non-stationary intervals with drastic wave height changes, traditional model predictions often show lag or significant deviation. Benefiting from the global field of view of the attention mechanism, this model more precisely responds to transient energy fluctuations caused by environmental mutations.

Case Study

Counterfactual Simulation & Optimization

Based on counterfactual deductions using real data from North Atlantic winter routes, the energy-saving potential of the weather-adaptive speed optimization strategy is verified.

Strategy Mechanism

Implementing an active load management strategy of "decelerating in high-resistance zones to avoid waves, and accelerating in low-resistance zones" to optimize energy efficiency under Just-In-Time (JIT) constraints.

Energy Saving

Simulation results show that this strategy achieves "5.54%"" fuel savings per voyage, equivalent to a reduction of approximately "956 kg" of fuel consumption.

Emission Reduction

A single voyage reduces carbon dioxide emissions by approximately "2.98 tons", providing a feasible solution for ships to meet CII rating requirements without hardware retrofitting.

Future Directions

Future Roadmap

Phase I: Multi-Physics Coupling & Expansion

Model Generalization & Vessel Adaptation

Expanding from the current single vessel type to container ships, tankers, and others, while incorporating more complex fluid-structure interaction effects to establish a general physics-guided energy efficiency model covering the entire fleet.

Phase II: Dynamic Decision Enhancement

Uncertainty Quantification & Robust Control

Introducing probabilistic forecasting mechanisms to quantitatively analyze the uncertainty of meteorological forecasts, upgrading from simple "energy consumption prediction" to "risk-aware" joint optimization of speed and route.
